That morning the paper had come and we studied it in silence for hours filling the time until the museum opened. This is what married people would do, I had thought. Our week-long relationship trial is going smoothly because here we are sitting in silence reading the paper and we’re both fine with that. He had noted a few weeks back that he liked spending time on his own, even while in the presence of another and I had breathed, because I was the same way. We seemed to have fallen into each other’s rhythms nicely and could do as we pleased without worrying if the other person felt shut out.
